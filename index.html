Conference paper: Investigating Wildfire Monitoring with Autonomous Flight Systems and UAVs

Abstract

  Wildfire monitoring is an essential component of effective wildfire management and for protecting environments, and human and animal lives.
  This article explores the key technologies driving autonomous systems such as AI, and advanced sensors which allow for the detection, monitoring, and assessment of wildfires focusing on thermal and optical sensing,
  real-time data processing, and swarm coordination. UAVs, which are equipped with thermal imaging technology which allows for the early detection of active fires and hotspots even in remote and uneven terrain.
  The integration of learning algorithms which enhances UAV autonomy which enables predictive modelling and dynamic monitoring of large-scale fires.
  This article also discusses the challenges associated with operating in low visibility conditions, monitoring air quality and environmental health after fires, and ensuring reliable communication with ground teams and response times.
